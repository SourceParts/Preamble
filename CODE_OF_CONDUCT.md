# Code of Conduct

This project exists because its contributors put work into it. The rules
below apply to everyone — maintainers included, especially maintainers.

## Expected behavior

- Engage with contributions on their technical merits.
- When rejecting a contribution, state a specific, substantive reason.
- Disagree with the code, not the contributor.
- Assume contributors are acting in good faith until shown otherwise.

## Unacceptable behavior

- Dismissing contributions without review.
- Labeling work ("slop," "noise," "spam," etc.) as a substitute for review.
- Personal attacks, harassment, or discriminatory language.
- Retaliating against contributors who raise concerns in good faith.

## AI-assisted contributions

AI tools are allowed. Contributors are responsible for understanding,
testing, and standing behind anything they submit, regardless of how it
was written. Contributions are reviewed on the merits of the code, not
on the tools used to produce it.

## If something goes wrong

If you believe a maintainer has violated this code of conduct, open an
issue tagged `conduct` or email <conduct@source.parts>. Concerns involving
a maintainer will be reviewed by the other maintainers, not the subject
of the complaint.

## Enforcement

Violations may result in: a private request to change behavior, a public
correction, temporary loss of commit or triage privileges, or removal
from the project.
